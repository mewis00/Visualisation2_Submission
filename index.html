Mass Shootings in USA from 1982-2022

Locations of Mass Shootings

Despite America's gunlaws allegedly promoting saftey and protection, the empiracal evidence suggests that it does quite the opposite.

There have been 100s of shootings over the years but this data set has captured 126 mass shootings between 1982-2022 which were the worst.
Locations such as Florida, California and Texas are some of the more common American states to have mass shootings, and generally they occur in more heavily populated locations

The most noteable mass shooting was the Las Vegas Strip Massacare in 2017 which had over 600 victims
84 cases involved the legal purchase of a firearm

79 of these cases involved semi-automatic rifles or handguns

The majority of mass shootings were conducted by a white male with mental health issues

Injuries vs Fatalities



This dataset classifies victims as someone who was injured or a fatality as a result of the crime

This visualisation does not include the Las Vegas Strip Massacare


The average number of fatalities is 8 people

The average number of injuries is 12 people

There is a linear relation between the two variables

Mental Health

38% of the cases in the dataset were caused by individuals with mental health issues who were able to legally purchase firearms. This could suggest that it's far too easy to lawfully acquire a firearm.

Regardless of mental health history, the majority of firearms were obtained lawfully, revealing the fallacy that is American gun laws.
1 in 5 Americans will experience a mental illness in a given year.

1 in 5 children, either currently or at some point during their life, have had a seriously debilitating mental illness.

1 in 25 Americans lives with a serious mental illness, such as schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, or major depression.

Gender Split

Mass shootings in America were almost primarily conducted by Males who were mostly able to obtain the firearms legally

65% of crimes were men who could legally purchase firearms Only 2 mass shootings were conducted with multiple perpetrators.
There have been 18 school shootings that are considered a mass shooting, but there are hunderds that had less than three direct victims and aren't captured in this data set.

More than half of American parents wrongly believe their child is unaware of their gun storage location at home. 70% of gun related school incidents involved a firearm taken from home, family or relative.

Race of Perpetrators

White Americans are the largest demographic to commit mass shootings at 53%, which coincidentally is the same as their % of the population from 2020 census data.

Black Americans have contributed to 16% of mass shottings captured in this data setwhich is slightly over their population percentage of 13.6. Black youths are 5 times more likely to die from gun violence than White Americans

Almost 80% - 19,384 out of 24,576 - of murders and suicides involved guns
